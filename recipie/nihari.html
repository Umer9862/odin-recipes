Nihari

Description

Nihari is a flavorful and slow-cooked stew that originated in South Asia. It is known for its rich and aromatic broth, tender meat, and a blend of traditional spices. Typically served as a hearty breakfast or a celebratory dish, Nihari has become a beloved comfort food. The stew is often prepared with beef or lamb, and the slow cooking process allows the flavors to meld, resulting in a deliciously tender and savory dish.

Ingredients

  • Meat:
    • Beef or lamb, cut into pieces
  • Bones: Beef marrow bones
  • Oil or ghee: 3-4 tablespoons
  • Onions: Sliced, 2 large
  • Garlic: Minced, 4 cloves
  • Ginger: Minced, 2 tablespoons
  • Spice Mix: Nihari masala or a blend of coriander, cumin, fennel, and other spices
  • Water: 6-8 cups
  • Flour: 2 tablespoons (for thickening, if needed)
  • Salt: To taste

Instructions

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a large pot. Sauté sliced onions until golden brown.
  2. Add minced garlic and ginger, and sauté for another 2 minutes.
  3. Add meat and bones to the pot, and brown them for a few minutes.
  4. Stir in the spice mix (Nihari masala) and cook for a couple of minutes until fragrant.
  5. Pour in water, bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for several hours until the meat is tender.
  6. If the stew needs thickening, mix flour with water to create a smooth paste and stir it into the pot. Cook until the stew reaches the desired consistency.
  7. Season with salt to taste.
  8. Serve hot, garnished with fresh coriander and ginger slices. Nihari is traditionally enjoyed with naan or rice.
