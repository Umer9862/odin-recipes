White Sauce Pasta

Description

White sauce pasta is a delectable Italian-inspired dish featuring pasta coated in a creamy and velvety white sauce. The sauce, also known as béchamel, is made by whisking butter, flour, and milk together to create a smooth, luxurious texture. Commonly infused with garlic for added flavor, this pasta dish is versatile and can be customized with various ingredients such as cheese, herbs, and vegetables. The result is a comforting and satisfying meal that combines the richness of the sauce with the simplicity of perfectly cooked pasta.

Ingredients

  • Pasta
    • Fettuccine
    • Penne
  • Butter: 2-3 tablespoons
  • All-purpose flour: 2-3 tablespoons
  • Milk: 2 cups (whole milk works well, but you can use low-fat or plant-based alternatives)
  • Garlic: 2-3 cloves, minced (optional for added flavor)
  • Salt and pepper: To taste
  • Nutmeg: A pinch (optional but adds a nice flavor)
  • Cheese: Grated Parmesan or any melting cheese of your choice (optional)

Instructions

  1. Boil the pasta according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.
  3. Add the minced garlic (if using) and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the all-purpose flour to create a roux. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly.
  5. Gradually whisk in the milk to avoid lumps. Continue whisking until the sauce thickens.
  6. Season the sauce with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg. Adjust the seasoning to taste.
  7. If using, stir in the grated cheese until melted and smooth.
  8. Combine the cooked pasta with the white sauce until well-coated.
  9. Garnish with fresh herbs and additional cheese if desired.
  10. Serve warm and enjoy your creamy white sauce pasta!
